Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

"The Missile Man of India"




  • Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was born on 15 October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu.
  • In his school years, he had average grades but was described as a bright and hardworking student who had a strong desire to learn.He spent hours on his studies, especially mathematics.
  • He attended St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli and graduated in physics in 1954. In 1955, he moved to Madras to study aerospace engineering in Madras Institute of Technology and graduated in 1960.
  • He joined the Aeronautical Development Establishment of the Defence Research and Development Organisation as a scientist after becoming a member of the DRDS. He was also part of the INCOSPAR committee.
  • He was transferred to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) where he was the project director of India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III).
  • He had first started work on an expandable rocket project independently at DRDO in 1965.
  • Between the 1970s and 1990s, Kalam made an effort to develop the Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV) and SLV-III projects, both of which proved to be successful.
  • He served as the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister and Secretary of the Defence Research and Development Organisation from July 1992 to December 1999.
  • He received Indira Gandhi Award for National Integration, Bharat Ratna in 1997, Padma Bhushan in 1981, Padma Vibhushan in 1990.
  • He served as the 11th president of Republic of India with an easy victory. His term lasted from 25 July 2002 to 25 July 2007.
  • After leaving office:
    • He became a visiting professor at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong, the Indian Institute of Management Ahmedabad and the Indian Institute of Management Indore
    • An honorary fellow of Indian Institute of Science, Bangalore
    • Chancellor of the Indian Institute of Space Science and Technology Thiruvananthapuram
    • Professor of Aerospace Engineering at Anna University
    • An adjunct at many other academic and research institutions across India.
    • He taught information technology at the International Institute of Information Technology, Hyderabad, and technology at Banaras Hindu University and Anna University.
  • He received 7 honorary doctorates from 40 universities.In 2012, he was ranked number 2 in Outlook India's poll of the Greatest Indian.
  • On 27 July 2015,he travelled to Shillong to deliver a lecture on "Creating a Livable Planet Earth" and only five minutes into his lecture, he collapsed and passed away of a sudden cardiac arrest.
